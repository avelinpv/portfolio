IKEA Assembly
In a course on Interaction Design, I together with 4 other students had a project focused on the design process. Our idea for IKEA Assembly came from the observed frustration from people having to assemble furniture. We saw that this was a widely spread problem.

Together, in the group, we had ideations and iterations on different ideas we had to solve the problem. Our final decision landed on an application that would incorporate animation to make it easier for the person to assemble a piece of furniture.

The whole project group took part in the development of the idea. In the later stages of developing the application, I and another student made the animation of the assembly of the furniture Marius.

memARy
In a course on Multimodal Interaction, me and 3 other students hade a project with a focus on combining two modalities. We chose to work with the visual and auditory modality, resulting in an AR application aimed to help training the memory.

We worked together in the researching stage and developing the frames for the project. As help during the development stage, we used some tools like Figma for design, Unity for development of the app and ARcore for the AR functionality. The main functionality of the app was to place 3D objects that represents something to remember, in a room and let the user walk a Memory Walk. The objects had each a connected sound that helped them memories a sequence of things.

I worked mainly on getting the AR application to work, coding in C# and implementing ARcore. The design frames developed by my group members was implemented in Unity and with its UI-objects we hade a clickable app with a workflow we were happy with. With Unity, I could also incorporate the distance detection so that the sound could be triggered when the user was close enough.

Duration: 12 weeks

Team size: 4

GoSaveThem
In a course on Software Development, me and 9 other students developed a game for a company with focus on teaching the public how ambulance staff worked. The game was later called GoSaveThem.

Together with the company, we developed during a half a year both the project plan and the game. The game was developed in the game engine Unity3D, which was new for almost everybody in the group. Using a asset package that the company gave us, we ueed the 3D models and implemented both the game mechanics and logic. The UI was developed by one team member, and the version control was contrelled through Github. The app was later deployed to AppStore.

During the project, I had contact with the company, modeld the world of the game and supported my teammates in the development while also contributing to the game logic.
